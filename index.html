The Unseen War: A Contract for the American Middle Class

Scott Jeffers 11-28-2025

We are standing at a breaking point. For the first time in American history, the next generation is on track to be less prosperous, less secure, and less free than the one that came before it. The "American Dream" - the simple bargain that hard work leads to a stable home, a secure retirement, and a better life for our children - has been dismantled.

We are told that our struggles are the result of the "other side" of the political aisle – or the outsiders trying to get in. We are told to fear others. But this is a lie. We are not fighting a war against each other; we are losing a war against a broken system.

This is a declaration of reality, a rejection of the distraction, and a call for a unified Middle Class Agenda.

I. The Economic Reality: The Broken Ladder

The financial math that built the American middle class no longer works.

  • The Asset Gap: We are competing against massive investment firms and global capital for basic human needs. Wealth has largely moved to the top 1%, leaving the middle class fighting over what is left.
  • The Inflation Tax: When the government prints trillions of dollars, it acts as a direct tax on the working class. Asset owners (stocks and real estate) see their wealth multiply with inflation, while wage earners see their purchasing power decrease. Inflation is a transfer of wealth from the young and the working to the old and the asset-rich. Meanwhile, our tax system treats money made by sweat differently than money made by passive assets. This undermines the dignity of work, creating a rigged game where wealth begets wealth while those who earn their living through labor are left behind.
  • The Erasure of Stability: We face a future with limited pensions, shaky Social Security, and growing debt. The "safety net" that allowed previous generations to take risks and build families is under assault.
  • The Inheritance Economy: We are creating an economy that rewards those who have and excludes those who want to work to gain. Some middle class families will inherit their parents' wealth, but it is increasingly difficult for new workers to enter this class through their own labor. And similarly difficult for the existing middle class to find their own new wealth. The ladder that once allowed upward mobility is being pulled out in favor of those who already climbed it.
  • The Illusion of Wealth: Even this "wealth" is often a trap for the middle class. Seniors find themselves "house rich but cash poor"- sitting on inflated home values while increasing property taxes and insurance premiums devour their fixed incomes. They cannot realize their asset gains without selling their homes, leaving them trapped in a situation where their primary asset becomes a financial burden rather than a source of security.

II. The Mechanism of Control: Rage and Division

Why do we not revolt against a system that is failing us? Because we are being systematically distracted.

The Media Trap

Mainstream and smartphone media exist not to inform us; they exist to sell ads. The business model is engagement. To keep us watching, they appeal to our most vulnerable human tendencies. They focus on "Culture War" issues - social outrage and political gossip - while ignoring the economic squeezing of the middle class. The media acts as a "red herring," directing our anger at each other so we do not direct it at the corporate-political marriage that creates our distress.

The "Divide and Conquer" Strategy

The greatest fear of the political establishment is a unified middle class. If conservatives and liberals realized we share the exact same economic enemies, we would constitute a supermajority capable of changing the system overnight.

To prevent this, the powers manufacture tribalism. They demand we identify as "Red" or "Blue" rather than "Working Class." They profit from gridlock. When Washington is deadlocked, the wealthy keep their tax breaks and monopolies while the rest of us fight a fake war.

III. The Middle Class Agenda

We must reject the status quo. We must reject the cynicism that says, "this is just how it is." We must propose a unification based not on identity politics, but on economic survival and human dignity. Our demands must not be radical; they must represent the baseline for a functioning free society.

What follows is a proposed Middle Class Contract. You may not agree with every specific solution listed here, and that is fine. In fact, that is the point. We should be debating how to solve these problems, rather than being distracted by what our screens tell us to hate. Let us fight fiercely over these details-because at least then, we are fighting about the things that actually determine our future.

IV. What Do We Want?

1. Education as an Investment, Not a Debt Trap

  • We want quality, affordable education.
  • Parents should be able to have faith in the public school system, which requires paying teachers a wage that commands respect and talent.
  • The era of crippling student debt should end. Education is the infrastructure of the future; we shouldn't tax our future innovators into poverty before they even begin.

2. Healthcare Freedom and Portability

  • No American should face bankruptcy due to a medical emergency.
  • Healthcare should provide the freedom to change jobs, start a business, or take risks without fearing for your life. Your health coverage should follow you, not trap you in a job you need to leave.

3. A True Safety Net

  • Welfare should exist for those who truly need it: the poorest, the disabled, and the elderly.
  • We should honor the contract of Social Security without burdening the young with insurmountable national debt.

4. Housing for Families, Not Hedge Funds

  • Housing should be for families to own and build wealth, not for hedge funds and investment firms to speculate and extract rent.
  • We should reform zoning laws and break the stranglehold of corporate landlords and investment firms on the housing market.
  • The middle class cannot thrive when housing costs consume half their income. Working families deserve the opportunity to own homes, not just rent from distant corporations.
  • Seniors who have paid off their homes should not be forced out by increasing property taxes. We should protect long-term homeowners from being taxed out of the homes they worked their entire lives to own.

5. Corporate Accountability and Fair Competition

  • We should demand enforcement of antitrust laws to break up monopolies that crush small businesses and inflate prices.
  • The corporate-political marriage should end. Lobbying should be transparent, and corporate influence should not outweigh the voices of working Americans.
  • The justice system should not be tiered based on economic status. Corporations and the wealthy should face the same consequences as individuals.

6. Infrastructure for a Free Economy

  • We should demand investment in physical and digital infrastructure that facilitates a strong, productive, free market. A crumbling country cannot house a thriving economy.

V. The Pledge

We must first recognize that the current media landscape is a tool to keep us divided. We must refuse to be manipulated. We must refuse to hate our neighbors for their political labels while our collective economic future is stolen. We must keep the conversation towards the issues that we care about. The issues that unify us.

It is easy to check out, to throw up our hands, and to let the decline happen. But can we choose to care? Can we choose to use our collective economic power and our voices to push for unification? I want the disjointedness to end. I want the middle class to reclaim its political power.

The real war is not Left vs. Right. It is the People vs. The Status Quo. And it is a war I hope we can win.
